## EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

Law7 is an MCP (Model Context Protocol) server providing AI assistants with access to legal documents. The AI handles explanation, simplification, and user guidance dynamically. This document focuses on data that MUST be in the database - data the AI cannot generate or substitute.

**Key Principle:** Only include data that:
1. Cannot be generated by AI (original legal documents)
2. Has authoritative value (official interpretations, court decisions)
3. Is not readily available elsewhere in better form (avoid duplicating Gosuslugi templates)

Based on MCP tool analysis:

### 1. REGIONAL LEGISLATION
**Priority: CRITICAL**  
**Reason: AI cannot invent regional laws; they exist only in official sources**

#### What to Add

**Regional Administrative Codes (КоАП субъектов РФ):**
- All 85 subjects of Russian Federation
- Essential because 70-80% of citizen legal questions involve regional administrative violations
- Examples:
  - Parking violations (each region has different rules and fines)
  - Noise regulations (hours vary by region)
  - Pet regulations (leash laws, vaccination requirements differ)
  - Environmental violations (local rules)
  - Trade and street vendors (local licensing)

**Key Regional Laws:**
- Social support programs (regional benefits vary significantly)
- Trade and commerce regulations
- Regional housing codes (where they exist)
- Local environmental protection
- Regional transport regulations

**Municipal Acts (Major Cities):**
- Moscow, Saint Petersburg
- Regional capitals (at least top 20 by population)
- Focus on:
  - Municipal administrative codes
  - Local regulations affecting daily life
  - Building codes and permits

### 2. JUDICIAL PRACTICE
**Priority: HIGH**  
**Reason: AI cannot know how courts actually interpret laws; this is authoritative interpretation**

#### What to Add

**Supreme Court of Russian Federation (Верховный Суд РФ):**

**Постановления Пленума (Plenary Resolutions):**
- Binding guidance on law interpretation
- Issued by full Supreme Court plenary sessions
- Have force of law interpretation
- Example: "О применении судами трудового законодательства"
- Frequency: 10-20 per year
- Must have: ALL plenary resolutions (approximately 500-1000 total)

**Обзоры судебной практики (Practice Reviews):**
- Supreme Court's analysis of how lower courts decide cases
- Identifies common errors
- Provides correct interpretation
- Published quarterly
- Must have: All practice reviews from 2015-present minimum

**Определения (Determinations):**
- Supreme Court decisions on specific cases
- Create precedents for similar cases
- Priority: Cases that clarify controversial legal issues

**Constitutional Court (Конституционный Суд РФ):**

**Постановления (Rulings):**
- Declarations of unconstitutionality
- Binding interpretation of Constitution
- Can invalidate laws or provisions
- Must have: ALL Constitutional Court rulings (approximately 500+ since 1991)

**Определения (Determinations):**
- Procedural decisions
- Often contain important legal positions
- Must have: Determinations with significant legal positions

**Legal Positions (Правовые позиции):**
- Constitutional Court's interpretations become binding
- Create constitutional doctrine
- Must be extracted and indexed

#### Data Structure

### 3. OFFICIAL INTERPRETATIONS
**Priority: HIGH**  
**Reason: Authoritative guidance from government agencies; AI cannot know agency positions**

#### What to Add

Government ministries and agencies issue official interpretations (письма, разъяснения) that clarify how laws should be applied. These are quasi-binding: while not laws themselves, they represent official government position and are followed by inspectors, tax authorities, courts.

**Key Agencies and Their Focus Areas:**

**Ministry of Finance (Минфин России):**
- Tax law interpretations (most important)
- Budget law clarifications
- Accounting standards guidance
- Frequency: 50-100+ letters per month
- Priority: HIGH (tax questions are very common)

**Federal Tax Service (ФНС России):**
- Tax procedure clarifications
- Tax benefit eligibility
- Tax form instructions
- Frequency: Regular updates
- Priority: HIGH

**Ministry of Labor (Минтруд России) / Rostrud:**
- Labor law interpretations
- Employment regulations
- Workplace safety standards
- Wage calculation rules
- Frequency: 20-50 letters per month
- Priority: HIGH (labor disputes very common)

**Rospotrebnadzor (Consumer Protection):**
- Consumer rights clarifications
- Product safety standards
- Service quality requirements
- Food safety regulations
- Frequency: Regular
- Priority: MEDIUM

**Ministry of Justice (Минюст России):**
- Legal entity registration
- Notary guidance
- Legal professional standards
- Frequency: Periodic
- Priority: MEDIUM

**Other Agencies:**
- Rosreestr (Real Estate Registry)
- GIBDD (Traffic Police) - regulations and clarifications
- Central Bank - financial regulations
- Various other regulatory bodies

#### What to Prioritize

**Tier 1 (Critical):**
- Minfin tax letters
- FNS tax guidance
- Rostrud labor clarifications
- Total documents: ~50,000-100,000 (last 5-10 years)

**Tier 2 (Important):**
- Rospotrebnadzor guidance
- Minjust instructions
- Rosreestr procedures
- Total documents: ~20,000-50,000

**Tier 3 (Lower priority):**
- Specialized agency guidance
- Technical standards
- Industry-specific regulations

### 4. TEMPORAL METADATA & CHANGE TRACKING
**Priority: MEDIUM-HIGH**  
**Reason: Legal accuracy requires knowing when laws are effective; AI cannot determine this from text alone**

#### What to Add

**Enhanced Effective Date Tracking:**
- Not just "effective from" but understanding transition rules
- Some laws apply to new cases only
- Some apply retroactively
- Some have grace periods
- Example: "Applies to contracts signed after 01.07.2026 but not to contracts signed before"

**Upcoming Changes Notification:**
- Laws passed but not yet in effect
- Scheduled amendments
- Sunset provisions
- Example: "This article will be amended on 01.01.2027 by Federal Law №456-FZ"

**Transition Rules:**
- How to handle cases that started under old law
- What happens to ongoing situations when law changes
- Grace periods for compliance

#### Enhanced Schema

-- Upcoming changes tracking table

    id UUID PRIMARY KEY,
    affected_document_id UUID,

    change_type VARCHAR(50),    -- amendment, repeal, new_article
    change_description TEXT,

    status VARCHAR(50),         -- scheduled, active, cancelled
    notification_created DATE,

    who_affected TEXT          -- "Все граждане", "Юридические лица", etc.

#### Data Source

Most of this comes from analyzing the amendment laws themselves:
- Federal laws often have Article 2 or final articles specifying transition rules
- Example: "Статья 2. Порядок вступления в силу настоящего Федерального закона"
- Requires NLP processing of amendment laws
- Can be partially automated, but requires careful review

### 5. CROSS-REFERENCES & RELATIONSHIPS
**Priority: MEDIUM**  
**Reason: Helps AI understand legal context and find related norms**

#### What to Add

**Explicit Citation Network:**
- Which articles reference which other articles
- Which laws reference other laws
- Hierarchical relationships (Constitution → Law → Regulation)

**Amendment Chains:**
- Article A was amended by Law X on date Y
- Then amended by Law Z on date W
- Full history of changes with sources

**Conflict Detection:**
- When two norms might contradict
- Resolution rules (newer law supersedes, special law supersedes general)
- Constitutional Court rulings on conflicts

**Thematic Grouping:**
- Articles dealing with same topic across different codes
- Example: "Statute of limitations" in GK RF, UK RF, KoAP RF

#### Data Structure

```sql
-- Citation relationships
CREATE TABLE legal_citations (
    id UUID PRIMARY KEY,
    citing_document_id UUID,
    citing_article VARCHAR(50),
    cited_document_id UUID,
    cited_article VARCHAR(50),
    citation_type VARCHAR(50),  -- references, amends, repeals, supersedes
    citation_context TEXT,
    identified_date DATE
);

-- Thematic relationships
CREATE TABLE thematic_groups (
    id UUID PRIMARY KEY,
    theme_name VARCHAR(200),
    theme_description TEXT,
    related_articles JSONB,     -- [{code: "GK_RF", article: "123"}, ...]
    keywords TEXT[]
);

-- Conflict tracking
CREATE TABLE norm_conflicts (
    id UUID PRIMARY KEY,
    document_a_id UUID,
    document_b_id UUID,
    conflict_description TEXT,
    resolution VARCHAR(50),     -- resolved_by_court, resolved_by_amendment, pending
    resolution_source UUID,     -- Constitutional Court decision or new law
    resolution_date DATE,
    status VARCHAR(50)          -- active, resolved
);
```

#### Generation Strategy

**Semi-Automated:**
- Parse legal text for explicit references (e.g., "в соответствии со статьей 123 ГК РФ")
- Track amendments through amendment laws
- Use NLP to identify implicit relationships

**Manual Curation:**
- Thematic groupings benefit from expert review
- Conflict identification often requires legal expertise
- Verification of automatically detected citations

## REMOVED FROM CRITICAL PATH

### Not Needed (AI Handles This)
1. **Simplified Explanations** - AI can explain legal text in plain language dynamically
2. **FAQ Database** - AI can answer questions based on legal documents; no need for pre-written answers
3. **Step-by-Step Procedures** - AI can guide users through processes based on laws

### Not Needed (Available Elsewhere Better)
4. **Document Templates** - Available on Gosuslugi (gosuslugi.ru) and official agency websites
5. **Calculators** - AI can calculate based on legal formulas; for complex cases, users should use official calculators
6. **Contact Information** - Readily available via search engines, official websites, Yandex Maps

### Low Priority for Now
7. **Court Decisions from Lower Courts** - Only Supreme Court and Constitutional Court for now
8. **Historical Laws** (pre-1991) - Focus on currently effective laws
9. **International Treaties** - Unless they create direct obligations for citizens
10. **Departmental Instructions** (Ведомственные инструкции) - Too specialized for general use

**3. Legal System Taxonomy:**

**Civil Law Countries (like Russia):**
- Code-based (Civil Code, Criminal Code, etc.)
- Hierarchical article structure
- Written constitutions
- Limited precedent
- Examples: Russia, Germany, France, Italy, Spain, Japan, China, Brazil
- Data structure: Codes → Parts → Chapters → Articles

**Common Law Countries:**
- Case law primary (precedents)
- Statutes secondary
- Judge-made law
- Examples: UK, USA, Canada, Australia, India, Hong Kong
- Data structure: Cases → Courts → Judges → Citations

**Mixed Systems:**
- Combination of civil and common law
- Examples: South Africa, Scotland, Quebec, Louisiana
- Data structure: Both codes and significant case law

- Can be freely collected, stored, redistributed
- Attribution to source required (pravo.gov.ru)

- Public domain in Russia

- Attribution recommended
